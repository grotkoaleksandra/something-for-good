Questions

Things people ask us.

Straight answers about who we support, how nominations work, and what happens next. If your question is not here, write to us and a person will reply.

Something For Good is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit that gives small grants directly to people.

Our primary mission is to recognize and support people who consistently demonstrate extraordinary generosity, service, and care for others — beyond their professional roles, beyond organizational responsibilities, and beyond what most people would reasonably expect.

We also help at those rare moments when a little support, given at exactly the right time, can change what happens next.

We generally support two kinds of people.

People who keep showing up for others in extraordinary ways. The teacher who spends evenings training rescue dogs to become companions for hospice patients. The handyman who provides free safety repairs and plumbing help for elderly neighbors. The Uber driver who gives free rides to families visiting loved ones in hospitals, often far from home. The neighbor who organizes a pop-up food pantry in her backyard. The mechanic who cuts and delivers free firewood to families struggling with heating costs. The young barber who gives free haircuts to veterans and to people preparing for their first day at a new job.

People facing those rare moments when a small amount of support could change what happens next. Creating a prom for high school students with intellectual disabilities after many were bullied or excluded from traditional events. Replacing tools stolen from a handyman who had spent years helping elderly neighbors for free. Replacing the camera an aspiring photographer sold to help pay family medical bills, so he could keep documenting his community and teaching others. Buying a ping-pong table that drew young people into a remote community center offering online counseling during a period of high youth suicide rates.

Most grants range from $1,000 to $2,500.

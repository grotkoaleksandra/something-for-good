Questions

Things people ask us.

Short answers, plainly given. If yours is not here, write to us and a person will reply.

Grants are between $1,000 and $2,500, given once, directly to one person. They are unrestricted, which means there are no conditions attached and no receipts to send back. The person who receives it decides what it is for.

Anyone quietly making life better for the people near them. We back two kinds of people: those who keep showing up for others over time, and those facing a hard moment when a small amount of money changes what happens next.

Grants go to individuals, not to organizations.

Nominations usually come from someone else, because the people we look for rarely put themselves forward. If you are in a hard moment and no one is going to nominate you, write to us anyway.

We read every nomination. If we move forward, we reach out to you first, then to the person you nominated. Vetting is short and respectful. The people closest to a community usually know who is holding it together, and we mostly confirm what they already see.

We are small, and we read nominations as they arrive. If we are moving forward we will contact you. If we are not, we will still let you know rather than leaving you waiting.

Tax treatment depends on individual circumstances, and we are not able to give tax advice. We recommend that recipients speak with a tax professional about their own situation.

Something For Good is a registered 501(c)(3) nonprofit, so donations are generally tax deductible to the extent allowed by law. Your own deduction depends on your circumstances, and a tax professional can tell you what applies to you.

Through individual donors who believe that good is worth backing. If you would like to give, or to fund a specific grant, write to action@somethingforgood.org.

Across the United States. Our leads come from teachers, nurses, social workers, and neighborhood organizers who already know who is making a difference where they live.
